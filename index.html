The Evolution of the Golf Ball
Chris Tenuta

Wooden Golf Balls


- Early 15th Century
- References about club and ball makers in Holland using wood balls in a game called "colf"
- Poor aerodynamics

Leather Hair Golf Ball


- Transitioned to leather shelled balls filled with hair

Featherie (1618)


- Wet goose feathers packed into horse or cow hide
- Dries, shrinks, paint, stamp
- Notable makers: Andrew Dickson, Leith and Henry Mills and St. Andrews

Smooth Guttie (1848)


- Created by Adam Paterson
- Made from rubber like sap from the gutta tree
- Easy to mold and remold / cheap to make
- Notable maker: Allan Robertson

Hammered Guttie (1880)


- People realized nicked Gutties performed better
- Started hammering patterns into them
- In 1890 started using machines to hammer (Bramble)
- Mass production by companies such as Dunlop

One Piece Haskell (1898)


- Universally adapted in 1901
- Rubber core wraped in thread with gutta percha cover
- Looked like a guttie but an extra 20 yards from the tee
- William Taylor created today's dimple pattern in 1905
